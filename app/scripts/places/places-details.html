Kalimpong

Spread in ribbons over a steep mountain ridge, surrounded by emerald-green tea plantations and towered over by the majestic Khanchendzonga (8598m), Darjeeling is the definitive Indian hill station. Dating back to the Raj era, it's arguably West Bengal’s premier attraction, and promises some interesting exploration for the curious traveller. When you aren’t gazing open-mouthed at Khanchendzonga, you can explore colonial-era architecture, visit Buddhist monasteries, and spot snow leopards and red pandas at the nearby zoo. On the steep and bustling streets winding through town, you can lose yourself amid an array of Himalayan faces from Sikkim, Bhutan, Nepal and Tibet. And finally, when energies start to flag, a good, steaming Darjeeling brew is never far away.

Mirik

Nestled near the Nepal border, halfway between Siliguri and Darjeeling, is this high-profile hill station and honeymoon destination. Mirik is surrounded by an undulating carpet of tea estates, orange orchards, cardamom plantations and forests of tall, dark Japanese cedars. It is a quiet retreat that remains off most visitors’ radars. Some of Mirik’s higher hilltops offer wonderful views of morning’s first light striking Khangchendzonga (8598m).

Kurseong

Kurseong, 32km south of Darjeeling, is a tiny and bustling hill town best known for its formidable league of tea estates and a rush of Raj-era boarding schools that dot its extents. Its name derives from the Lepcha word khorsang, a reference to the small white orchid prolific in this area. Flanked by hilly slopes draped with manicured tea estates, it is also currently the southern terminus for the charming toy trains of the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway.

Digha Mandarmani

DIGHA/ Mandarmani, a seaside resort village in the eastern Indian state of West Bengal, lies in East Midnapore district and at the northern end of the Bay of Bengal. It is one of the large and fast developing seaside resort villages of West Bengal. It is almost 180 km from Kolkata off the Kolkata – Digha highway. Red crabs crawling around the 13 km long beach is a special attraction of Mandarmani.

Shantiniketan

Santiniketan is a small town near Bolpur in the Birbhum District of West Bengal and about 212 kms north of Kolkata. It is famous due to Nobel laureate Rabindranath Tagore, whose vision became what is now a University town – Visva-Bharati University. The place now attracts thousands of visitors each year. Santiniketan was earlier called Bhubandanga (named after Bhuban Dakat, a local Dacoit), and was owned by the Tagore family. In 1862, Maharishi Devendranath Tagore, the poet’s father, while on a boat journey to Raipur, came across a landscape with red soil and lush green paddy fields. He decided to plant more saplings and built a small house. He called his home Santiniketan. He founded an ashram here in 1863 and became the initiator of the Bramho Samaj.

Sunderban

Sundarbans is the largest single block of tidal halophytic mangrove forest in the world. This region is densely covered by mangrove forests. The Sundarbans forest is the home for various birds and crocodiles, spotted deer, water monitor, the gangetic and Irrawaddy dolphins are found in the water of the Sundarbans, The mud flats, sands dunes and sand flats are home to a huge variety of crabs, mud skippers and is one of the largest reserves for the Royal Bengal tiger The Sundarban forest Is just a few hours distance from Kolkata, India. Sundarban is a must trip for all lovers of nature, forests, wild animals, Sand the gentle waves of the rivers. This experience will leave you with lifetime memories Sundarban is one of the finest national park in the world. Its varying climatic regions allows for an abundant variety of flora, fauna and wildlife. Typical sightings in Sundarbans boat cruise including deer, leopard, monkeys, snakes, alligators and crocodile, migratory birds and of course the elusive Royal Bengal tiger will make your tour more adventurous and memorable and at the same time you may have a chance to see the tiger during Sundarban boat cruise. Sundarban is also a paradise for birdwatchers that includes such rarities as the Masked Fin foot, Mangrove Pitts and the Mangrove Whistler. The Sundarbans are a part of the world’s largest delta formed by the Mighty Rivers Ganges, Brahmaputra and Meghna. A Sundarbans boat cruise into the jungle adds an extra adventure to the Sundarbans tour. Resort is located right opposite of the Sundarbans Tiger Reserve, at the edge of lush mangrove forest of the Sundarbans.

Boi Para (local market for books)

College Street or the ‘Boi Para’is the largest used book market in the world and the largest book market in India which would awe you by its collection of rare books. It is not uncommon to find original copies of rare masterpieces for a bargain. "The Book Worms” walk takes you along the countless book kiosks, the Bengali publication houses, the lanes of residential alleys, one of the oldest education institution in India- Presidency College and the most famous hangout place in Kolkata – `Coffee House’.
